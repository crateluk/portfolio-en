Vellum Simulation

This section brings together two cloth simulation projects built with Vellum, Houdini's integrated physics solver. The first project is the simulation of a dress inspired by the iconic looks of Anya Taylor-Joy: the garment was modelled in Marvelous Designer, textured in Adobe Substance Painter, and simulated in Houdini with the Vellum solver to achieve fluid movement and convincing fabric folds. The second explores the organic simulation of a blooming flower, developed entirely inside Houdini using procedural systems and cloth dynamics.

Dress Simulation

The second project is the simulation of a dress inspired by the iconic looks of Anya Taylor-Joy, known for her refined style with strong couture and fantasy influences. The garment was designed and modelled in Marvelous Designer, using its native cloth simulation to define the fit and draping of the fabric. Textures were created in Adobe Substance Painter, with particular attention to silk and satin materials. The dress was then exported to Houdini, where the Vellum solver managed the final dynamic simulation, delivering fluid movement and natural folds consistent with the physical properties of the fabric.

The workflow involved careful panel modelling in Marvelous Designer to build the garment respecting real-world proportions. The Substance Painter pass produced detailed PBR materials with wear layers and fabric colour variations. In Houdini, the imported geometry was integrated with the Vellum system for the final simulation: cloth constraints with mass, damping and friction parameters calibrated to replicate the behaviour of silk on a moving character.

Blooming Flower

The first project is an organic cloth simulation built entirely in Houdini. The goal was to reproduce the natural process of a flower opening, using the Vellum solver to drive the dynamics of each petal. Starting from procedural geometry, custom stretch and bend constraints were configured for every petal layer, resulting in a progressive and convincing opening motion. The entire production — from geometry and shading to final rendering — stayed within Houdini, leveraging its procedural nature to iterate quickly on the aesthetic result.

The simulation leverages Vellum constraints to handle inner and outer petals differently, enabling a gradual opening with distinct timing and rotation angles for each layer. The Houdini node network allows parametric control over opening speed, cloth stiffness, and the animation profile, making it straightforward to adjust and adapt the effect to other flower types.
